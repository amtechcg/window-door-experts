Answers Before You Ask

Most single-family homes with 8–15 windows are completed in a single day. Larger homes or complex custom work may take two days. We'll give you a realistic timeline during your in-home estimate so you can plan accordingly.
In most New Jersey municipalities, yes — permits are required for window and door replacements that change the opening size or affect the building envelope. We handle the permit application process for you as part of every project. There are no hidden permit fees on our end.
We work with leading manufacturers including Andersen, Pella, Simonton, Marvin, and ProVia. We'll recommend the right product for your budget, climate exposure, and aesthetic — not just the most expensive option. We're brand-agnostic because our job is to find the best fit for you.
An insert replacement (also called a pocket replacement) slides a new window into the existing frame. It's faster and less disruptive — a good choice if your frames are in solid condition. A full-frame replacement removes everything down to the rough opening — necessary if the framing, sill, or exterior casing is rotted or damaged. We'll assess which is right for your home during the estimate.
ENERGY STAR certified windows can reduce heating and cooling costs by 12–30% depending on your current windows, home size, and climate zone. NJ homeowners replacing single-pane windows typically see the largest savings — sometimes $200–$600 per year on a full home. We can estimate your projected savings during the consultation.
We offer a lifetime warranty on our installation labor. Products carry their own manufacturer warranties — typically 10 years to lifetime depending on the brand and product line. If anything is wrong with the installation workmanship, we fix it at no charge. Period.
